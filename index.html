Information About Morocco

Morocco is a North African country known for its rich culture, deep history, and strong national identity. The country stretches from the Mediterranean Sea to the Atlantic Ocean and includes diverse landscapes such as mountains, deserts, and historical cities.

Morocco's independence was officially declared on 18 November 1956, marking the end of the French protectorate. This day is now celebrated annually as the Fête de l'Indépendance.

General Information

  • Capital: Rabat
  • Largest City: Casablanca
  • Languages: Arabic & Tamazight (official)
  • Independence: 18 November 1956
  • Currency: Moroccan Dirham (MAD)
  • Government: Constitutional Monarchy
  • King: Mohammed VI

Historical Facts About Morocco’s Independence

The Moroccan independence movement began long before 1956. Nationalists fought politically and diplomatically to end foreign rule. Sultan Mohammed V played a major role in unifying the people and negotiating freedom.

  • 1912 – Beginning of the French Protectorate.
  • 1930 – The Berber Dahir increases national resistance.
  • 1944 – Independence Manifesto signed.
  • 1953 – Exile of Sultan Mohammed V, causing nationwide uprisings.
  • 1955 – Return of Sultan Mohammed V.
  • 1956 – Official Independence of Morocco.

Key Figures

  • Mohammed V: Father of Moroccan Independence.
  • Hassan II: Leader who modernized Morocco after independence.
  • Allal El Fassi: Leader of the Istiqlal Party.
  • Ahmed Balafrej: Key diplomat during negotiations.
